PlayAI x Meta Superintelligence Lab

From the looks of it around the industry today, the integration of voice AI has become a necessary requirement of nearly all AI chat, agents, and applications. However, this wasn’t the consensus opinion just two years ago.  While we had basic NLP, ASR, and text-to-speech models, the most frequently-used applications were in outdated IVR systems for call centers, primitive transcription apps, and some seemingly-always-in-beta virtual assistant apps on phones.

Today, we have incredible advancements in the frontier speech model world of TTS, STT, and even Speech-to-Speech models which reduce latency tremendously and increase emotional understanding, depth, and delivery.  And no consumer LLM feels complete without true multi-modality, with voice input and output, and image and video understanding and generation.  Voice agents are live and in-production in both the enterprise and consumer, in over 50 languages at fidelity and speed benchmarks that far exceed anyone’s expectations as of just 24 months ago.

Just over a year ago, we met one of the great founder teams in the space: PlayAI.  I first learned about PlayAI from Jeremy Werner, an ex-Apple software engineer who was at Humane (an early AI hardware innovator in voice as UI) at the time, and also from my friend, Garry Tan, who was using their app to clone his voice and let you have a pretty hilarious uncanny conversation with his digital voice twin.  After going through YC, Mahmoud Felfel and Hammad Syed had been training some initial TTS models that had found early traction with developers and enterprises, but gleaned two insights from this early phase of the company: there was an opportunity to pre-train a variety of new speech models beyond just Whisper-like TTS models, including turn-taking conversational dialogue models, diffusion-based speech models, and faster and more genuinely human speech-to-speech models, and there was also a vacuum in high-quality voice AI products for creators and consumers.  Felfel and Hammad saw an opportunity to build out the lego blocks for Voice AI agents while many other model labs were focused on inference APIs only, and to build out a creator studio for consumers who wanted to use their lightning-fast speech models.

I think many of the startups that will succeed in this Generative AI era, will look much like PlayAI – a hybrid of research engineering and product design/development.  We led their combined $21M seed round alongside prior investors, Race Capital, 500 Global, and YC in the middle of 2024 to go after this dual opportunity to train novel speech models but also dive into building more interface and workflow for the consumer/prosumer. We helped guide a former Kindred Ventures portfolio founder, Kei Yoshikoshi, to join PlayAI as their head of product engineering and help push the envelope on what could be achieved on the product side. Within a year after this seed financing, PlayAI onboarded thousands of customers and users, and reached eight figures in revenue – all by a small team working out of a non-descript street level office in downtown Palo Alto while competing with many larger growth stage companies, extremely well-funded large frontier model labs, and Big Tech.

So today, after months of discussions between Meta Superintelligence Labs and PlayAI, we are finally able to officially congratulate the founders and the entire team at PlayAI!  We’re stoked for them as it became immediately clear that a great match exists between Felfel and Hammad’s vision on the most conversational AI models and products, and Mark’s objectives for deeply personal AI available to everyone. We’ve really enjoyed the late sunday night product jam sessions, their minimal board presentations which were mostly fantastic product demos, healthy arguments about training compute budgets, voice agent demos at our AGMs and Kindred Founder Summit using Kanyi’s voice and mine in embarrassing ways, and most of all the “what-if” energy and passion they brought to their work constantly.

Big congratulations from all of us at Kindred Ventures on your acquisition and merger with Meta’s new Superintelligence Labs!  It was a pleasure and honor to be your investor and board member on this journey. We’re calling first dibs to get early access to your speech models and voice AI products now that you’ll be training and building with all of those many SIL gigawatts and GPUs you’ll have access to now. 🙂
