Salesforce Picks Up Talent from Closing AI Startup Moonhub

By Thomas Morgan

Published June 03, 2025
Updated June 05, 2025

AI startup Moonhub have recently announced that they will be joining forces with Salesforce to help them accelerate their AI developments.

While many believed that it was another acquisition made by Salesforce, a company spokesperson confirmed that this is not the case. Moonhub is winding down their operations, and Salesforce are taking on the majority of the company’s workforce.

Who Are Moonhub?

Founded in 2022 by Nancy Xu, a former Meta engineer and Stanford AI lab researcher, Moonhub worked to change the recruitment process for companies by developing AI agents that could autonomously source, vet, and engage potential job candidates. 

Their flagship product, AI Recruiter, proved to be a huge success with their clients, with the company claiming to have achieved a 50% reduction in time-to-hire, while 80% of candidates found by Moonhub reached the interview stage (per Maginative). This tool was designed with a “human-in-the-loop” approach, which ensured that while Moonhub gathers all the information, the decision-making remains in the hands of the people.

Moonhub have served hundreds of companies, including firms in the Fortune 500 and other startups like Thrive Global and Activeloop. Since 2022, Moonhub has raised $14.4M from investors such as Khosla Ventures, GV (formerly Google Ventures), and Salesforce themselves, who were one of Moonhub’s early investors.

Xu is also a well-reknowned name in the AI space, and was named one of Time Magazine’s 100 Most Influential People in AI for 2023.

How Will Moonhub Help the Agentforce Effort?

As Salesforce continues to ramp up their Agentforce efforts, adding the deep expertise of the Moonhub team should prove to be a big plus as they continue scaling their AI product.

Made up of ex-Stanford Computer Science PhDs and MBAs, as well as ex-Twitter Cortex and Meta ML leads, the former employees of Moonhub are exactly the type of people who will help lead Salesforce in the right direction.

However, Salesforce have taken on a lot of talent as of late from their recent acquisitions. Much of their purchasing focus has been on AI startups – from buying Convergence.ai in May, Zoomin for $450M last September, and Own Company for around $1.9B (also in September last year). Their new talent pool is strong, but it raises questions around how the company can use all of this expertise effectively and ensure that everyone is on the same page.

Final Thoughts

All in all, this appears to be an ideal situation for Salesforce. They already have an existing relationship with Moonhub, and given Moonhub’s small team size, integration should be fairly straightforward.

Salesforce is racing against companies like Microsoft, Google, and OpenAI to lead the market in enterprise AI agents with Agentforce. Acqui-hiring teams such as Moonhub’s highlight just how competitive the battle for AI talent has become. 

Salesforce hopes Moonhub’s talent will speed up their AI projects, potentially positioning the company as a leader in the AI agent economy – a market CEO Marc Benioff clearly believes in.
