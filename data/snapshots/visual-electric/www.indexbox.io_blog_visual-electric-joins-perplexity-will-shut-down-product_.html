Visual Electric Joins Perplexity, Will Shut Down Product

The AI design startup Visual Electric, which was backed by Sequoia, announced it is joining search startup Perplexity. The company noted on its site that the team would be joining a new Agent Experiences group at Perplexity.

Perplexity's CEO, Aravind Srinivas, confirmed the acquisition with a post on X. The terms of the deal were not disclosed.

As part of the transition, Visual Electric will shut down its product in 90 days. The startup said users will be able to export their data, and subscribers will get prorated refunds.

Visual Electric was founded in 2022 by Colin Dunn, a former Facebook and Dropbox employee, Adam Menges, a former Apple employee, and Zach Stiggelbout, a former Microsoft engineer. The tool, aimed at designers, let users create images and apply edits on an infinite canvas.

The idea was to have designers build ideations and concepts over a whiteboard. Over time, the company added support for video generation. The startup had raised $2.5 million in funding from Sequoia, BoxGroup, and Designer Fund.
